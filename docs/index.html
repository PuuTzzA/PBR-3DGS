3D Gaussian Inverse Rendering with Diffusion Priors

Thomas Putzer, Lukas Jochim
Technical University of Munich
Code Paper Poster

Abstract

Gaussian Inverse Rendering (GIR) decomposes multi-view images of a scene into 3D Gaussians carrying Physically Based Rendering (PBR) materials—such as albedo, metallic, roughness—along with learned environment lighting. However, optimizing this decomposition solely through a photometric loss leads to a severe albedo-lighting ambiguity: the environment map and surface albedo can trade off brightness and color freely, resulting in baked-in illumination and physically incorrect material properties.

We resolve this ambiguity by introducing a prior-guided supervision pipeline that leverages both multi-view ground-truth priors and diffusion-model priors (using NVIDIA DiffusionRenderer). By combining these with a multi-phase training schedule and a custom Light-Linear Indirect Illumination (LLI) model, our pipeline guides geometry optimization, aligns normals, and separates reflectance features cleanly. The result is a robust, physically accurate factorization that enables high-fidelity relighting under novel environment maps.

Method overview. Our pipeline integrates multi-view supervision across a multi-phase curriculum. First, a Geometry Warm-up phase initializes the scene using flat albedo priors. Second, a Normal Alignment phase enforces consistency of the surface geometry. Finally, a Full PBR Optimization stage coordinates the materials with our Light-Linear Indirect Illumination (LLI) model, scaling specular and diffuse indirect terms based on environment map radiance for energy-consistent relighting.
